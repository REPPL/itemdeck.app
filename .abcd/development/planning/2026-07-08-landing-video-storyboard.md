# itemdeck — 90-second tutorial storyboard & script

A gentle, friendly walkthrough for complete beginners. Warm and a little nostalgic, never technical. British English throughout. **No jargon** — the words "repository", "schema" and "JSON" never appear. GitHub is only ever "free hosting for your collection", and only if it comes up at all.

**Total runtime:** ~90 seconds
**Voice:** one warm, unhurried narrator (mid-pace, smiling)
**Music:** soft, upbeat, slightly retro (think a warm synth pad with a light shuffle) — kept low, fading under the voice. Nothing plays with sound until the viewer presses play.
**On-screen style:** the real product, captured cleanly, with a light VHS/tracking-line flourish on the intro and outro title cards only — never so heavy it obscures the interface.

---

## Scene 1 — Welcome (0:00–0:12)

**On screen:** Title card. The itemdeck logo, a small fan of cards drifting into place. A faint VHS tracking line rolls once and settles. Behind it, a soft off-white (or charcoal in dark mode) backdrop with a subtle halftone.

**Voiceover:**
> "Got a collection you love? Retro games, trading cards, records, anything at all. itemdeck brings it to life — right here in your browser."

**Motion notes:** Cards fan out with an easy, confident curve. Title settles. Keep it calm; this is the "well-made board game box" opening, not a game-show intro.

**Accessibility:** Open captions burned in from the first word. Title card readable at AA contrast.

---

## Scene 2 — Load a demo collection (0:12–0:35)

**On screen:** Cursor clicks a friendly **"Try a demo collection"** button. The screen fills with a tidy, responsive grid of cards — a mixed set so no single hobby dominates (a few game cartridges, some trading cards, a couple of records). Cards arrive with a gentle shuffle-in.

**Voiceover:**
> "Start with a ready-made demo — no account, nothing to install. In a blink, your collection becomes a neat deck of cards you can actually browse."

**Motion notes:** Cards cascade in over ~0.6s with a soft stagger. Pause a beat on the full grid so the viewer takes in the whole set.

**Accessibility:** Narration describes what's appearing on screen, so the moment lands even with sound off or eyes away.

---

## Scene 3 — Flip a card (0:35–0:55)

**On screen:** The cursor hovers a single card; a soft glow appears. A click, and the card turns over with a satisfying flip to reveal its details on the back — name, year, a line or two. Then a quick beat showing search: a word is typed and the grid narrows to matching cards.

**Voiceover:**
> "Tap any card and it flips — there's the story on the back. Looking for one thing in particular? Just start typing, and everything else steps aside."

**Motion notes:** One clean flip, unhurried (~0.7s). For the search beat, let two or three cards fade back so the narrowing reads clearly. Don't rush.

**Accessibility:** Flip and filter are shown slowly enough to follow. Captions carry the meaning for anyone who can't see the fine text on the card.

---

## Scene 4 — Play a round of Memory (0:55–1:20)

**On screen:** A tab or button labelled **"Play"**; the grid rearranges into a Memory board of face-down cards. The cursor turns one card, then another — no match, they turn back. Turns a third, then its pair — **match!** The two cards get a little celebratory pop and a soft chime.

**Voiceover:**
> "And here's the fun part — your collection *is* the game. This is Memory: find the matching pairs. There's a guess-the-value game and stat duels too, all built from the cards you already treasure."

**Motion notes:** Show exactly one near-miss and one match, so it's easy to follow. The match "pop" is small and warm, not a fireworks display. Keep the chime gentle.

**Accessibility:** A short on-screen note ("Match!") accompanies the audio cue, so the win is clear without sound.

---

## Scene 5 — Your turn (1:20–1:30)

**On screen:** Back to the fanned deck from the opening, now with a warm outro title card. A single button glows: **"Try a demo collection"**. A light VHS tracking line rolls once and fades. The itemdeck logo and web address settle at the end.

**Voiceover:**
> "That's itemdeck — free, and ready when you are. Bring your own collection to life. Have a play."

**Motion notes:** Mirror the opening so the film feels bookended. End on a still, readable frame (logo + URL) held for ~2 seconds.

**Accessibility:** Final call-to-action is on screen as text, not just spoken. Frame held long enough to read comfortably.

## Production checklist

- **Length:** trim to land at or just under 90 seconds; the script above paces to roughly that.
- **Captions:** open (burned-in) captions throughout, plus a separate caption file for the embedded player.
- **Sound:** starts muted; plays only on the viewer's press. Music low under voice; a single soft chime on the match, nothing jarring.
- **Reduced motion:** offer a "calm" cut with cross-fades instead of card flips/shuffles for viewers who prefer less movement.
- **Themes:** film once in light and once in dark so the embed can match the visitor's chosen theme.
- **No jargon:** verify the final voiceover and captions never say "repository", "schema" or "JSON". If hosting comes up, it's "free hosting for your collection" only.
- **Accuracy:** every feature shown or spoken must exist in the app as shipped — the game mechanics are Memory (matching pairs), a guess-the-value round, and stat duels; the layouts are Grid, List, Compact and Fit-to-Viewport. No invented mechanics or views.
- **Tone check:** if a moment makes someone smirk *at* the product rather than smile *with* it, cut it.
