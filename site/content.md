### hero.lede
itemdeck turns your collection into a beautiful, interactive deck of cards — right in your browser. Browse it, search it, show it off, and actually play with it.

### how.intro
No jargon, no setup wizardry. If you can share a link, you can make a deck.

### step1.body
Paste a link to a collection and itemdeck loads it instantly. Your data stays yours.

### step2.body
Flip through a tidy grid, search by anything, and sort by category, year or maker.

### step3.body
Turn your own collection into a round of Memory, a game of Guess the Value, or a Top Trumps duel.

### feature.views.body
The same collection, four ways. A responsive grid for the big picture, a list with room for the detail, a compact view for browsing big collections, and Fit-to-Viewport for the full-screen showcase. Cards flip with a satisfying turn to reveal the details on the back.

### feature.discovery.body
Start typing and the grid narrows to match. Filter by category, platform or year, and group your cards however makes sense to you. Big collections stop feeling overwhelming the moment you can slice them.

### feature.games.body
Because it is already a deck of cards, itemdeck can play with it. Match pairs in Memory, test your knowledge in Guess the Value, or go head-to-head Top Trumps style comparing stats. Same cards you admire, now a game night.

### audience.collectors
Bring order to the shelf. See everything at a glance, sort it the way your brain does, and admire the whole set the way it deserves.

### audience.curators
Tell a story with a themed set — a best-of, a museum wing, a birthday gift — and share it with a single link that just works.

### audience.players
Turn a rainy afternoon into a game. Memory rounds, guessing games and stat duels, all built from the cards you already treasure.

### cta.title
Let's bring your collection to life

### cta.body
Open a ready-made demo and have a play — no account, no install, nothing to lose.

### hosting.body
Your collection is stored using GitHub — a free, reliable home for your files on the web. Think of it as a tidy online folder that itemdeck reads from. You don't need to be technical: our step-by-step guide walks you through it, and once it's set up, sharing is as simple as sending a link.
